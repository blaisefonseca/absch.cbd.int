YOU are the editor at Witchcraft Review. Witchcraft Review is not a Checkpoint.

One day as you are editing your latest review when a USER walks into your office and shows you a very interesting research paper on a notoriously rare and magic bean. You would love to publish the paper in your magazine.

You agree to publish the paper in your journal. The article will come out in the following weeks magazine.

As an appointed checkpoint for the Nagoya Protocol, you ask the USER to provide information on prior informed consent, the source of the genetic resource, the establishment of mutually agreed terms, and/or the utilization of genetic resources. You secretly hope that he will have the IRCC, as it would make your job of filling the form for the checkpoint communique easier.

The user provides you with one of the following options. YOU MUST CHOOSE:

The IRCC (This user didnt not recieve an IRCC but since this is a only game you can choose it to see what will happen)
The user provides the national permit. (This user did not recieve a national permit but since this is a only game you can choose it to see what will happen)
The user only tells you the magic beans in the research paper come from Middle-earth

You have been tasked to do a SUBSTANTIAL EXAMINATION. You carefully look at all the information presented by the user and YOU MUST CHOOSE:

That everything is in order and that you can go ahead and publish the paper
That you cannot publish the paper.
